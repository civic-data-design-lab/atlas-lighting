METHODOLOGY

In order to select geographically diverse and economically vibrant set of U.S. cities to best represent the variation of urbanized areas in the country, the team conducted a selection process. Typically, research at this scale focuses on the top 100 MSAs (Metropolitan Statistical Area) by population. This convenient metric accounts for a large portion of the nationâ€™s populous and economy. The research uses one primary cutoff (population) and several factors (pop. growth, pop.-weighted density, and Gross Metropolitan Product) to sort the list of potential MSAs for further study.

The plot helps reveal a system of MSAs that separate into at least five identifiable groups. The following 5 selection sets will serve as the primary source of the final selection to be determined by data compatibility/availability (see plot figure for full groups). The sets were determined by selecting the highest, lowest, and the median 2010 population-weighted density MSA from each group (listed in that order below).
